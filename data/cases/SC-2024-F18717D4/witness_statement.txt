SINGAPORE POLICE FORCE
WITNESS STATEMENT
============================================================
Case Reference: SC-2024-F18717D4
Date Recorded:  2024-09-08

STATEMENT
----------------------------------------
On 8 September 2024, I was sitting in the food‑court at Jurong East MRT when I saw a chap in a hoodie pulling out his laptop and typing fast near the coffee shop – later found out he was Ravi a/l Murugan, the alleged fraudster. He kept tapping on his phone, receiving a bunch of SMS alerts that looked like they were from an online shop I’d just shopped at, and I thought it was strange but didn’t think much of it. A few days after, the complainant from Block 212 called the police saying her online banking had been drained of over S$12,000 after she clicked a phishing email that pretended to be from that shop. The police later raided his HDB flat in Block 105 and seized a laptop and a mobile phone, confirming he was part of a small cyber‑fraud ring targeting shoppers around Jurong East. I’m still shocked lah, because we all just go about our day and never expect something like that to happen right in our neighbourhood.
