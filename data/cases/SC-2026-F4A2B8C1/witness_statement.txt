STATEMENT OF LIM BEE HOON

I am a 62-year-old retired teacher. I live alone at Block 44, Bedok North Avenue 3.
My daughter Lim Shu Ling visits me on weekends.

On the morning of 10 June 2026, around 10am, I was at home having breakfast when
my phone received a WhatsApp message. The number showed "+65 9123 4567". I did not
recognise the number, but the message said it was from "DBS Bank Fraud Prevention".
There was a DBS logo as the profile picture.

The first message said my DBS account had been used for suspicious overseas
transfers. I was very scared because I keep most of my savings there — about
$120,000. The message said I needed to act fast or I would lose everything.

A few minutes later, the same number called me on WhatsApp. A man spoke to me in
English with a slight accent. He said his name was "David Tan" and that he was a
senior fraud officer at DBS. He sounded very professional and calm. He told me he
could see my account activity in real time and that there were 7 unauthorised
transactions totalling $98,000 being processed by hackers overseas.

He told me the only way to protect my money was to transfer it to a "safe account"
that DBS had set up for emergencies. He said the account was insured by MAS. He gave
me a NRIC number to use for PayNow — he said it was a DBS internal account.

I was very worried. I transferred $15,000 first to test, as he instructed. He
confirmed receipt and said it arrived safely. He told me to transfer the rest as
quickly as possible before the hackers could take it.

I made a second transfer of $18,000 at around 12 noon. After that, a different
person spoke to me. He said he was "Officer Rahman from the CPF Board" and warned
me that the same hackers had also tried to access my CPF savings. He told me I
might be arrested if I did not cooperate because the hackers might be using my
account for money laundering and SPF could think it was me.

I was terrified. I have never been in trouble with the law in my life. The man said
as long as I cooperated and transferred my remaining money for safekeeping, the
police would know I was a victim and not a criminal. He also told me I should not
tell my family or the police until the investigation was over, or I could be charged
for obstructing justice.

I made the third transfer of $14,000 at around 2pm.

After 2pm, the WhatsApp calls stopped. By 3:30pm, I was feeling very uneasy. I had
not heard from "David Tan" or "Officer Rahman" again despite calling the number back.
The messages were read but there was no reply.

When my daughter arrived at 3:45pm, I told her what happened. She immediately said
it was a scam. She called DBS bank on the official hotline and confirmed there was
no such fraud unit officer and no such transfer out of my account by hackers.

I want to add that I am angry and embarrassed. I am not a stupid person. I was a
teacher for 35 years. But the way these people spoke to me was very convincing. They
knew my name. They knew my account ending numbers. They made me feel like I would
be arrested if I did not cooperate. I felt I had no choice.

I am willing to provide my phone for forensic examination. I have not deleted any
messages.

Signed: LIM BEE HOON
Date: 11 June 2026

STATEMENT OF RAJAN KUMAR S/O MURUGESAN (Money Mule — Suspect)
Recorded by    : Inspector Ng Wei Liang
Date / Time    : 11 June 2026, 14:30 hrs
Interpreter    : Not required
Caution        : Administered under CPC s 23

I am 22 years old and currently serving National Service in the Singapore Army
at Pasir Laba Camp. I am on a 2-day off-in-camp duty cycle.

About three weeks ago, I saw an advertisement on Telegram in a group chat called
"SG Extra Income 2026". The post said I could earn $300-$500 per day just by
allowing someone to use my bank account for "international business payments". The
post said it was legal because the money was from overseas clients paying for
e-commerce products.

I replied to the Telegram username "@agent_casey88". He sent me instructions via
Telegram. He told me to provide my DBS account number, my iBanking login, and a
photo of my NRIC. I sent these. I know now that this was wrong. He told me the
transfers would come in and I should forward them out within one hour to a Maybank
account in Malaysia that he would give me each time.

On 10 June, I received a Telegram message from @agent_casey88 with the Maybank
account number. He told me to expect three incoming transfers and to forward them
immediately. Each time money came in, I transferred it out within 30 minutes to the
Maybank account he gave me. I kept $500 as my fee as agreed.

I did not know the money was from a scam victim. I thought it was e-commerce
business money. I have since blocked @agent_casey88 and that Telegram group.

I have cooperated fully. I am willing to testify.

Signed: RAJAN KUMAR
Date: 11 June 2026
