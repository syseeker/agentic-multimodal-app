2. INCIDENT SUMMARY

On 10 June 2026 at approximately 10:14 hrs, the victim Ms Lim Bee Hoon received a
WhatsApp message from a number displaying a Singapore prefix (+65 9123 4567). The
sender claimed to be "Officer David Tan" from DBS Bank's Fraud Prevention Unit.

The message stated that Ms Lim's DBS savings account (ending -3812) had been
flagged for suspicious overseas transactions totalling SGD 98,000, and that her
funds were at immediate risk. The sender directed Ms Lim to "secure her funds"
by transferring them to a "protected DBS escrow account" provided by the caller.

Over the course of approximately 4 hours (10:14 hrs to 14:22 hrs), Ms Lim made
three PayNow / FAST transfers totalling SGD 47,000 to a DBS account registered
to one Rajan Kumar (NRIC [REDACTED]), a 22-year-old Singaporean student.

At 15:30 hrs, Ms Lim contacted her daughter, who immediately recognised the
incident as a scam. Ms Lim filed a police report at Bedok Police Post at 16:45 hrs.

4. OFFENDER / SUSPECT PROFILE

Primary suspect identity is unknown. The WhatsApp number +65 9123 4567 has been
traced to a VoIP service provider (Twilio, US-registered) with no corresponding
Singapore SIM registration. The number is believed to be operated from overseas,
possibly Malaysia or China.

During the scam, the suspect employed the following social engineering tactics:
  a. Initial DBS bank impersonation (spoofed caller ID)
  b. Escalation to "CPF Board officer" (claimed funds also at risk in CPF)
  c. Escalation to "SPF Police Officer" (threatened arrest if victim did not comply)
  d. Urgency and fear tactics: told victim NOT to speak to family or risk arrest
  e. Screen-share request (rejected by victim) via WhatsApp video call

6. IMMEDIATE ACTIONS TAKEN

  a. SPF Anti-Scam Centre (ASC) notified at 16:52 hrs — account freeze request
     submitted to DBS Bank for account xxxx-xxxx-3391
  b. DBS confirmed partial fund recovery: SGD 2,500 retained in Rajan Kumar's
     account at time of freeze. SGD 44,500 had already been transferred out.
  c. Immigration and Checkpoints Authority (ICA) flagged on Rajan Kumar for
     travel restriction.
  d. Request for Mutual Legal Assistance (MLA) to be submitted to Malaysian
     authorities for the Maybank account that received SGD 44,500.
